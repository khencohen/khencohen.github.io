Research

What I work on

I'm drawn to problems that sit at the boundaries between fields. Much of my work focuses on quantum photonic computing — designing robust quantum gates in integrated circuits, building spatial mode transformers, and developing quantum key distribution protocols. I'm particularly interested in how ideas from robust control can make photonic quantum devices practical and scalable.

In parallel, I study quantum many-body physics (complexity diagnostics, scrambling, localization transitions) and foundational questions in machine learning theory (why gradient-based learning works, high-dimensional classification). I find that moving between these areas often leads to unexpected connections — methods from one field becoming tools in another.

Quantum Information

Many-body localization, scrambling diagnostics, graph state fusion, and quantum switching protocols.

Quantum Optics & Photonics

Integrated quantum gates, directional couplers, spatial mode sorting, optical neural networks, and hybrid QKD.

AI & Learning Theory

Linearization principles in neural networks, Gaussian mixture classification, sparse coding, and generative models.
