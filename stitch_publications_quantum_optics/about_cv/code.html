Dr. Elena Thorne

Quantum Optics & Nanophotonic Systems

My research operates at the intersection of coherent light-matter interaction and scalable quantum architectures. Currently based at the Max Planck Institute for the Science of Light, my work focuses on developing high-fidelity entanglement gates within semiconductor nanophotonic circuits.

Prior to my current appointment, I completed my postdoctoral fellowship at MIT, where I explored topological photonics for robust quantum information transport. I am deeply interested in the emergence of non-classical correlations in open quantum systems and the practical limitations of decoherence in near-term quantum devices.
